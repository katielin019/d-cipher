Solving tips

  • The most common letters in the English language are E, T, A, I, O, and N
  • Try to spot double-letter patterns, i.e. 'OO' in BOOT
    • The most frequent double-letter combinations are 'EE', 'LL', 'SS', 'OO', 'TT', 'FF', 'RR', 'NN', 'PP', and 'CC'
    • Some letters almost never appear as doublee-letter patterns, e.g. 'XX' or 'JJ'
    • For double-letter patterns that appear at the end of a word, the most likely letters are 'EE', 'LL', 'SS', and 'FF'
  • One letter words are almost always A or I; in rare cases, it may be O
  • If your puzzle contains any punctuation, use its positioning to your advantage
    • If you have a 4-letter word with a contraction between letters 3 and 4, it's likely to be a word containing "N'T"
    • If it's a longer word with a contraction between its second-to-last and last letters, the last letter is probably "S"
